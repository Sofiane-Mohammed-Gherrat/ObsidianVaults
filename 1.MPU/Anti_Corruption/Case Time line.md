# Timeline of the 1MDB and SRC International Scandal (2009–2024)

## 2009 — Creation of 1MDB

The scandal begins when the Malaysian government created the state investment fund **1Malaysia Development Berhad (1MDB)** shortly after **Najib Razak became Prime Minister.

The fund was intended to **promote economic development and attract foreign investment** in Malaysia. Najib chaired its advisory board and held strong influence over its operations. ([Dawn](https://www.dawn.com/news/1963501?utm_source=chatgpt.com "Former Malaysian PM Najib Razak convicted in 1MDB graft trial - World - DAWN.COM"))

---

## 2011 — Creation of SRC International

A subsidiary called **SRC International** was established under 1MDB to invest in energy and natural resources.

SRC later obtained **RM4 billion in loans from the Malaysian government pension fund (KWAP)**. The funds were intended for strategic investments but were later linked to financial misconduct. ([AML Network](https://amlnetwork.org/aml-news/najib-razak-gets-15-year-jail-in-1mdb-corruption-case/?utm_source=chatgpt.com "Najib Razak Gets 15-Year Jail in 1MDB Corruption Case"))

---

## 2012–2014 — Large Financial Transfers

During this period, billions of dollars were raised through international bonds and financial transactions linked to 1MDB.

Investigators later alleged that **around $4.5 billion was misappropriated through shell companies and offshore accounts** connected to the scheme. ([Dawn](https://www.dawn.com/news/1963501?utm_source=chatgpt.com "Former Malaysian PM Najib Razak convicted in 1MDB graft trial - World - DAWN.COM"))

Some of the money was reportedly used to purchase luxury assets, real estate, art, and finance the Hollywood film _The Wolf of Wall Street_. ([Dawn](https://www.dawn.com/news/1963501?utm_source=chatgpt.com "Former Malaysian PM Najib Razak convicted in 1MDB graft trial - World - DAWN.COM"))

---

## 2015 — Scandal Becomes Public

In 2015, investigative journalists revealed that **hundreds of millions of dollars linked to 1MDB had been transferred into Najib’s personal bank accounts**.

This triggered international investigations involving several countries including the United States, Switzerland, and Singapore. ([Dawn](https://www.dawn.com/news/1963501?utm_source=chatgpt.com "Former Malaysian PM Najib Razak convicted in 1MDB graft trial - World - DAWN.COM"))

The scandal severely damaged public trust in the Malaysian government.

---

## 2018 — Political Change and Criminal Charges

The controversy contributed to Najib’s defeat in the **2018 Malaysian General Election**, ending more than 60 years of rule by his political coalition.

Soon after the election, Malaysian authorities **formally charged Najib with corruption and money laundering offences related to SRC International and 1MDB funds**. ([AML Network](https://amlnetwork.org/aml-news/najib-razak-gets-15-year-jail-in-1mdb-corruption-case/?utm_source=chatgpt.com "Najib Razak Gets 15-Year Jail in 1MDB Corruption Case"))

---

## 2019 — Trial Begins

Najib’s first trial began in the Kuala Lumpur High Court and focused on **RM42 million transferred from SRC International into his personal accounts**.

Prosecutors argued that he **abused his power as Prime Minister and Finance Minister to facilitate these transactions**.

---

## 2020 — First Conviction (SRC International Case)

In July 2020, the High Court convicted Najib of **seven charges**:

- abuse of power
    
- criminal breach of trust
    
- money laundering
    

He received **12 years in prison and a RM210 million fine**. ([Encyclopedia Britannica](https://www.britannica.com/event/1Malaysia-Development-Berhad-scandal?utm_source=chatgpt.com "1Malaysia Development Berhad scandal | Scheme, Jho Low, Najib Razak, Exposure, & Legacy | Britannica"))

This made him **the first former Malaysian prime minister convicted of corruption**.

---

## 2022 — Appeals Rejected and Imprisonment

Malaysia’s Federal Court rejected Najib’s final appeal, confirming the conviction.

He began serving his prison sentence at Kajang Prison in August 2022.

---

## 2024 — Partial Royal Pardon

In 2024, Malaysia’s pardons board **reduced Najib’s prison sentence**, cutting it roughly in half, though the conviction remained. ([Encyclopedia Britannica](https://www.britannica.com/event/1Malaysia-Development-Berhad-scandal?utm_source=chatgpt.com "1Malaysia Development Berhad scandal | Scheme, Jho Low, Najib Razak, Exposure, & Legacy | Britannica"))

Meanwhile, other related 1MDB cases and civil lawsuits to recover stolen assets continued.

# Why This Timeline Matters

The timeline shows that the scandal evolved in **three major phases**:

1. **Creation and misuse of public funds (2009–2014)**
    
2. **Exposure and global investigations (2015–2018)**
    
3. **Trials and convictions (2019–2024)**
    

Overall, investigators believe **billions of dollars were diverted from the fund**, making the 1MDB scandal one of the largest financial corruption cases in modern history. ([Dawn](https://www.dawn.com/news/1963501?utm_source=chatgpt.com "Former Malaysian PM Najib Razak convicted in 1MDB graft trial - World - DAWN.COM"))
