
## 1. The "7 Charges" Logic

**Question:** _"Why was he charged 7 times for only RM42 million? Isn't that double-counting?"_

- **Your Answer:** "No, it is not double-counting because each charge represents a different **criminal act** under different laws.
    
    - The **Abuse of Power** charge was for the _act of approving_ the RM4 billion loan to get a bribe.
    
    - The **3 CBT charges** were for the _act of dishonestly moving_ the money out of SRC.
    
    - The **3 Money Laundering charges** were for the _act of receiving and using_ that illegal money in his personal accounts.
    
    - The law treats the 'theft' and the 'spending' of the money as separate offences."
    

## 2. The AMLATFAPUAA 2001

**Question:** _"Why do we use such a long name for the money laundering act?"_

- **Your Answer:** "It stands for the **Anti-Money Laundering, Anti-Terrorism Financing and Proceeds of Unlawful Activities Act 2001**. It was originally just 'AMLA,' but it was amended in 2014 to be more comprehensive. In this case, Najib was charged under **Section 4(1)(b)**, which specifically deals with receiving or using proceeds from an illegal activity."


## 3. The Sentence & Fine

**Question:** _"He was sentenced to 12 years and 10 years for different charges. Why is he only serving 12 years?"_

- **Your Answer:** "The High Court Judge, Justice Nazlan Ghazali, ordered the sentences to run **concurrently** (at the same time), not consecutively (one after another). Therefore, the longest sentence—12 years for abuse of power—is the total time served."

- **Follow-up on the Fine:** "The **RM210 million fine** is based on the MACC Act, which mandates a fine of at least **five times** the value of the bribe (RM42 million × 5 = RM210 million)."


## 4. The "Arab Donation" Defense

**Question:** _"How did the court disprove his claim that the money was a gift from King Abdullah of Saudi Arabia?"_

- **Your Answer:** "The court found the 'donation' defense to be 'implausible' and an 'afterthought' for three reasons:
    
    1. The money came from **SRC International** (Malaysian public funds), not Saudi Arabia.
    
    2. The BBM (BlackBerry Messenger) chats showed Najib knew the funds were being managed by Jho Low to ensure his checks didn't bounce.
    
    3. He never officially declared this 'donation' to the Cabinet or the government at the time."

## 5. Why the Federal Court? (The 3 Levels)

**Question:** _"Why did the trial take so long (2018–2022)?"_

- **Your Answer:** "In Malaysia, a criminal case of this magnitude goes through three levels to ensure a fair trial:
    
    1. **High Court (2020):** Convicted him.
    
    2. **Court of Appeal (2021):** Upheld the conviction, calling the case a 'national embarrassment.'
    
    3. **Federal Court (2022):** The final stage, where 5 judges unanimously dismissed his final appeal."
