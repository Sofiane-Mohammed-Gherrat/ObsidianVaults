Here is a **clear explanation of Najib Razak, his role in the SRC International case, the investigations, allegations, and the offences he was convicted of.**

---

# 1. Who Najib Razak Is:

Najib Razak is a Malaysian politician who served as:

* **Prime Minister of Malaysia (2009–2018)**
* **Minister of Finance**
* Chairman of the **1MDB advisory board**

During his time in office, he created and oversaw the state investment fund **1Malaysia Development Berhad (1MDB)**, which was <mark class="hltr-myblue">intended to promote economic development and investment</mark> in Malaysia. However, the fund later became the centre of one of the **largest corruption scandals in modern history**, with billions of dollars allegedly misappropriated. ([Reuters][1])

The **SRC International case** is one of the criminal cases linked to this wider scandal.

---

# 2. What SRC International Is

SRC International was a <mark class="hltr-mygreen2">subsidiary</mark> of the 1MDB fund.[[What is Subsidiary]]

>It was initially created to invest in energy and natural resources projects. The company received **RM4 billion in loans from the Malaysian government’s Retirement Fund (KWAP)**. ([Malay Mail][2])

However, investigators later discovered that part of this money had been **diverted through complex financial transactions and transferred into Najib Razak’s personal bank accounts.**

---

# 3. Najib Razak’s Role in the Case

At the time of the events:

* Najib was **Prime Minister**
* He was also **Finance Minister**
* He had influence over **SRC International’s decisions and funding**

Prosecutors argued that he **used his position of authority to approve government-backed loans for SRC International and later benefited from those funds personally.** ([BusinessToday][3])

Because of his positions, he had **direct control or influence over the company and the public funds involved**.

---

# 4. The Investigations

Investigations into the 1MDB and SRC scandals began around **2015** after reports by journalists and financial investigators raised questions about suspicious financial transfers.

The investigations involved several institutions:

* Malaysian Anti-Corruption Commission (MACC)
* Bank Negara Malaysia (central bank)
* Malaysian police
* International law enforcement agencies

Authorities traced financial transfers through multiple companies and bank accounts.

Investigators discovered that **RM42 million from SRC International was transferred into Najib’s personal bank accounts at AmIslamic Bank.** ([Malay Mail][4])

Witness testimony, financial records, and banking documents were used to reconstruct how the money moved through several intermediary companies before reaching his accounts.

---

# 5. Allegations Against Najib Razak

Prosecutors alleged that Najib:

1. **Approved government-backed loans for SRC International**
2. **Allowed company funds to be diverted**
3. **Received RM42 million from SRC International into his personal accounts**
4. **Used his position of power to facilitate these actions**

The prosecution argued that this constituted **abuse of power and corruption involving public funds**.

Najib consistently denied wrongdoing and argued that he was **misled by advisors and believed the money was a donation from Saudi Arabia**, but the court rejected this defence.

---

# 6. The Charges Against Him

In the SRC International trial, Najib faced **7 criminal charges**:

### 1. Abuse of Power (1 charge)

* Using his position as Prime Minister and Finance Minister for personal benefit.

### 2. Criminal Breach of Trust (3 charges)

* Misusing public funds entrusted to him.

### 3. Money Laundering (3 charges)

* Moving illegally obtained funds through financial transactions to conceal their origin.

These charges were brought under Malaysian laws including:

* Malaysian Anti-Corruption Commission Act
* Penal Code (Section 409)
* Anti-Money Laundering Act.

([Malay Mail][4])

---

# 7. Conviction and Sentence

In **July 2020**, the Kuala Lumpur High Court found Najib **guilty on all seven charges**. ([Malay Mail][4])

The court concluded that the prosecution had proven beyond reasonable doubt that:

* the money came from SRC International
* it was deposited into Najib’s personal accounts
* he abused his authority for personal gain.

### Original sentence

* **12 years imprisonment**
* **RM210 million fine**

([The Straits Times][5])

His appeals were rejected by higher courts, and he began serving his sentence in **2022**.

Later, a **royal pardon in 2024 reduced his sentence to six years and reduced the fine to RM50 million**, although the conviction remained. ([BusinessToday][6])

---

# 8. Importance of the Case

The SRC International case is historically significant because:

* It was the **first time a former Malaysian prime minister was convicted of corruption**.
* It demonstrated that **even high-ranking political leaders can be prosecuted**.
* It exposed governance weaknesses in government-linked companies.

The case also became a key example used in discussions of:

* political accountability
* anti-corruption governance
* judicial independence.
