# 1. “Weak Oversight Structures”

### What “oversight” means

In corporate governance, **oversight** means the mechanisms that monitor decisions and prevent misuse of money. These usually include:

- Board of Directors
- Internal auditors
- Financial controls
- Compliance systems

Their role is to **check whether decisions are legal, financially sound, and transparent**.

---

### What went wrong in the SRC case

In SRC International, these oversight systems **did not properly challenge decisions or detect suspicious transactions**.

For example:

- Directors testified that the board often **followed instructions linked to Najib’s authority** because he was simultaneously Prime Minister, Finance Minister, and adviser to SRC. ([The Sun Malaysia](https://thesun.my/news/malaysia-news/src-board-followed-najibs-orders-believing-it-was-for-country/?utm_source=chatgpt.com "SRC board followed Najib's orders believing it was for country"))
    
- Some directors said the board was effectively reduced to **approving the shareholder’s directions rather than independently deciding company strategy**. ([Malay Mail](https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2024/06/06/src-board-reduced-to-rubber-stamping-najibs-directions-ex-director-says/138426?utm_source=chatgpt.com "SRC board reduced to rubber-stamping Najib’s directions, ex-director says | Malay Mail"))
    

This means the board **did not function as an independent supervisory body**, which is its main governance role.

---

### Why this is a governance failure

In a well-governed company, the board should:

- question risky financial decisions
- verify large transactions
- ensure compliance with regulations
- stop conflicts of interest

If the board simply approves decisions without scrutiny, **fraud or mismanagement becomes much easier**.

# 2. “Lack of Institutional Independence”

This refers to **the independence of public institutions that are supposed to investigate or prevent corruption.**

Examples include:

- Anti-corruption agencies
- Prosecutors
- regulators
- government audit bodies

These institutions must be able to **act without political pressure**.

---

### Why this was an issue in the scandal

The 1MDB/SRC scandal raised concerns that some institutions were **too influenced by political power while Najib was still in office**.

Because Najib held multiple powerful roles:

- Prime Minister
- Finance Minister
- advisor to SRC International

there were fears that **investigations or oversight bodies might hesitate to challenge decisions made by the head of government**.

---

### Why independence matters

If anti-corruption institutions are not independent:

- corruption investigations may be delayed
    
- evidence may not be pursued
    
- officials may fear political retaliation
    

After the scandal became public, many experts and reform advocates in Malaysia called for **stronger institutional safeguards to ensure investigators and prosecutors can act freely**.

---

# Simple way to understand the difference

|Problem|Meaning|Example in SRC case|
|---|---|---|
|Weak oversight structures|Internal governance failed|Board did not challenge transactions|
|Lack of institutional independence|External watchdogs may be politically constrained|Anti-corruption institutions faced pressure when investigating top officials|
