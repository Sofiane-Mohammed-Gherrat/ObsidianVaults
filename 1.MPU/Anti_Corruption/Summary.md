# Governance Failures Highlighted in the Case

### 1️⃣ Political Control Over Corporate Entities

Najib held multiple roles and had **significant influence over SRC International’s decisions**, meaning transactions could occur with limited independent oversight. ([The Star](https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2025/08/15/najib-betrayed-publics-trust-src-international-wants-rm42mil-back-says-counsel?utm_source=chatgpt.com "Najib betrayed public’s trust, SRC International wants RM42mil back, says counsel | The Star"))

---

### 2️⃣ Weak Board Oversight

Some directors testified they **did not know about the RM42 million transfers**, indicating failures in internal governance and reporting mechanisms. ([The Sun Malaysia](https://thesun.my/news/malaysia-news/courts/former-src-director-denies-knowledge-of-rm42-million-transfer-to-najib/?utm_source=chatgpt.com "Former SRC director denies knowledge of RM42 million transfer to Najib"))

---

### 3️⃣ Lack of Financial Monitoring

The funds moved through **multiple companies before reaching personal accounts**, demonstrating weak financial control systems.

---

### 4️⃣ Use of Layering to Hide Money Trails

Routing money through intermediary companies is a **classic money-laundering technique** used to obscure the origin of funds. ([Yahoo News Malaysia](https://malaysia.news.yahoo.com/rm42m-transfer-src-misappropriation-najib-104418305.html?utm_source=chatgpt.com "RM42m transfer from SRC was misappropriation by Najib as he was sole benefactor, appellate court told - Yahoo News"))

---

# Simplified Flow of the Fraud

```
Public Funds → Government-linked company → Intermediary companies → Personal accounts
```

This structure allowed funds to **appear legitimate at each step**, making the corruption harder to detect until investigators reconstructed the financial trail.
