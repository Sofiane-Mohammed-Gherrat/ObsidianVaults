
For your **MPU3322 presentation**, the "Key Lessons and Preventive Measures" section is your chance to shine in **CLO 3 (Identifying values in current issues)**. To get those "Good" or "Excellent" marks, you need to move beyond simple ideas like "don't steal" and focus on **systemic reforms**.

Here are 5 expanded keys with explanations that you can use for your slides:

## 1. Separation of Executive Roles

- **The Lesson:** In the SRC case, Najib Razak held the positions of **Prime Minister and Finance Minister** simultaneously. This created a "power vacuum" where the person approving the funds (Finance Minister) was the same person overseeing the entire government (PM).
    
- **Preventive Measure:** Implement a legal ban on the Prime Minister also holding the Finance Portfolio. This ensures that the Ministry of Finance can act as an independent "check" on the Prime Minister’s spending requests.
    

## 2. Institutional Independence (The "Watchdogs")

- **The Lesson:** The trial revealed that early investigations were stalled because the heads of the MACC and the Attorney General (AG) were appointed directly by the Prime Minister. This allowed for "political interference."
    
- **Preventive Measure:** * **Independent MACC:** Change the appointment process so that the MACC Chief is appointed by a **Parliamentary Select Committee**, not just the PM.
    
    - **Separation of AG and Public Prosecutor:** The AG (who advises the government) should be a different person from the Public Prosecutor (who decides who to charge). This prevents a PM from "firing" a prosecutor who tries to investigate them.
        

## 3. Governance of Government-Linked Companies (GLCs)

- **The Lesson:** SRC International was a GLC. The case showed that "Shadow Directors" (like Jho Low) and political figures could bypass the Board of Directors by claiming they were acting on the PM's "orders."
    
- **Preventive Measure:** * **No Political Appointments:** Only professionals, not politicians, should sit on the boards of GLCs.
    
    - **Mandatory Asset Declaration:** All high-ranking GLC officials and MPs must declare their assets to an independent body annually to detect "unexplained wealth."

## 4. Regulating Political Financing

- **The Lesson:** A major defense in the trial was the "Arab Donation" narrative—the claim that the RM42 million was a political gift. This highlighted a massive loophole: Malaysia currently has no specific law regulating how much money a politician can receive as a "donation."
    
- **Preventive Measure:** Enact a **Political Financing Act**. This would require all political donations to be channeled through a regulated fund, with the names of donors made public (transparency) and limits on how much a single person or company can give.
    

## 5. Protection of Whistleblowers and Civil Servant Integrity

- **The Lesson:** Many officers at SRC and AmBank saw "red flags" but were too afraid to report them. As one court judgment noted, the instructions were carried out because civil servants lacked the protection or the "spine" to say no to a superior.
    
- **Preventive Measure:** * **Strengthen the Whistleblower Protection Act 2010:** Ensure that employees who report their bosses are 100% protected from losing their jobs or facing lawsuits.
    
    - **Integrity Training:** Move beyond "box-ticking" training and implement the **ISO 37001 (Anti-Bribery Management System)** across all government departments.

# Version 2


## 1. Mandatory Separation of Executive Power

- **The Lesson:** The "SRC scandal" happened because the Prime Minister was also the Finance Minister. This concentration of power allowed one person to bypass the Ministry of Finance's internal checks and balances, personally approving a RM4 billion loan for a company he controlled as "Advisor Emeritus."
    
- **Preventive Measure:** Legally mandate that the **Prime Minister cannot simultaneously hold other ministerial portfolios**, especially the Ministry of Finance. This creates a "horizontal check" where the Finance Minister must answer to the Prime Minister, and vice versa, preventing a single individual from having total control over the national budget and government-linked companies (GLCs).
    

## 2. Reform of the Appointment Process (The "Three Watchdogs")

- **The Lesson:** In 2015, when investigations into 1MDB/SRC were heating up, the sitting Attorney General was removed and replaced. This sent a message that enforcement heads were not "bulletproof" against political pressure.
    
- **Preventive Measure:** **Parliamentary Oversight of Appointments.** Instead of the Prime Minister advising the King on who should be the MACC Chief or the Attorney General, these candidates should be vetted and approved by a **Parliamentary Special Committee on Corruption**. This ensures they have "bipartisan" support and are loyal to the Constitution, not the person who hired them.
    

## 3. Protection of "Gatekeepers" (Civil Servants & Auditors)

- **The Lesson:** Evidence in court showed that several civil servants and bank officers felt something was wrong but were told to "follow orders from the top." In the SRC case, the "top-down" culture of obedience overrode professional integrity.
    
- **Preventive Measure:** **Strengthen the Whistleblower Protection Act 2010.** Current laws often only protect you if you report to a specific agency; they should be expanded to protect any employee who refuses to follow an illegal order. Additionally, the **National Audit Department** should be made an independent body reporting directly to Parliament, rather than being a department under the Prime Minister's Office.
    

## 4. Regulation of Political Financing

- **The Lesson:** A central defense in the Najib trial was the "Arab Donation" claim—the idea that the RM42 million was a gift for political use. Because Malaysia had no law requiring the disclosure of political donations, this "grey area" was used as a legal shield.
    
- **Preventive Measure:** Enact a **Political Financing Act**. This law would make it mandatory for all political parties and candidates to declare the source of every ringgit they receive. It would also set a "ceiling" (limit) on donations to prevent wealthy individuals or companies from "buying" political influence.
    

## 5. Corporate Liability (Section 17A of the MACC Act)

- **The Lesson:** SRC International was used as a vehicle for corruption, but for a long time, the organization itself faced no consequences—only the individuals did.
    
- **Preventive Measure:** Implementation of **Section 17A of the MACC Act (Corporate Liability)**. This law (which came into force in 2020 partly because of these scandals) means that if an employee commits bribery, the **entire company and its directors** can be held criminally liable unless they can prove they had "Adequate Procedures" in place to prevent it.

    _"Before 2020, companies could claim they didn't know their employees were bribing officials. Now, under Section 17A, the 'Failure to Prevent' corruption is a crime itself. This forces companies like SRC to have strict internal audits."_

    _"We learned that the MACC needs to be independent. If the Chief of MACC is worried about being fired by the Prime Minister, they cannot investigate fairly. Moving appointments to Parliament protects the 'integrity' of the investigators."_
