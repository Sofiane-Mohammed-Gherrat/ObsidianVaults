# 📖 2. DEFINITIONS OF CORRUPTION

### ❗ KEY POINT

- **No universal definition**

---

### Important Definitions (VERY TESTABLE)

#### World Bank

- Abuse of **public office** for private gain  
    👉 Only public sector

---

#### UNDP (United Nations Development Program)

- Misuse of <mark class="hltr-mygreen2">public power</mark> for <mark class="hltr-mygreen2">private benefit</mark>  

Includes:
- Bribery
- Extortion
- Fraud
- Nepotism
- Embezzlement

---

#### Transparency International (MOST IMPORTANT)

- Misuse of **entrusted power** for private gain  
    👉 Covers:
    
- Public + private sector
    

---

### Active vs Passive Bribery
?
- **Active** → giving/offering bribe
- **Passive** → accepting/requesting bribe

# 💰 4. GRATIFICATION (VERY IMPORTANT)

### KEY TERM

- Law uses **“gratification”**, not “bribe”

### Forms include:

- Money
- Gifts
- Loans
- Discounts
- Services (even sexual)
- Positions
- Bonuses
- Property

### ⚠️ 👉 IMPORTANT:  
<mark class="hltr-mygreen2">✔️ Not just cash</mark>

# ⚖️ 5. MAIN OFFENCES (SUPER IMPORTANT)

## 🔴 4 TYPES (MEMORIZE SECTIONS)

---

## 1. S.16(a) → Accepting bribes (Passive)

- <mark class="hltr-mygreen2">Receiving</mark> or asking for gratification

✔️ Anyone can commit  
✔️ Common: public officers

---

## 2. S.16(b) → Giving bribes (Active)

- <mark class="hltr-mygreen2">Giving</mark>/offering/promise

✔️ Often private sector

---

## 3. S.18 → False Claims

- <mark class="hltr-mygreen2">Submitting fake documents</mark>

Examples:

- Fake receipts
- Fake medical certificate
- Fake overtime

---

## 4. S.23 → Abuse of Power

- Public officer uses position for:
    
    - Self
    - Family
    - Associates

👉 Example:

- Awarding contract to relative

---

# 🏛️ 7. PUNISHMENT FOR CORRUPTION

### For:

- S16(a), S16(b), S23

👉 Punishment:

- Jail ≤ 20 years
    
- Fine:
    
    - **5× bribe OR RM10,000 (whichever higher)**

---

### For S18 (False Claims)

- Same structure

---

### ❗ CRITICAL RULE

✅ MUST be:

- <mark class="hltr-myyellow">Jail AND fine</mark>  
    ❌ NOT “or”

---

### Additional Consequences

- Public servant:
    - <mark class="hltr-mygreen2">Lose pension</mark>

- Company director:
    - <mark class="hltr-mygreen2">Disqualified</mark>

---

### Seizure
- Temporary control

### Forfeiture
- Permanent loss (court order)

# 💸 9. MONEY LAUNDERING

### Definition
- <mark class="hltr-myyellow">Making illegal money look legal</mark>

---

### 3 STAGES (VERY IMPORTANT)

<mark class="hltr-mygreen2">Placement</mark> → <mark class="hltr-mygreen2">Layering</mark> → <mark class="hltr-mygreen2">Integration</mark>

#### 1. Placement
- Put money into system

#### 2. Layering
- Hide origin (multiple transfers)

#### 3. Integration
- Use money legally (buy assets)

---

👉 MCQ FAVORITE:  
Order of stages

---
# 🏛️ PUNISHMENT FOR MONEY LAUNDERING
?
- <mark class="hltr-myyellow">Section 4(1) AMPLATFAPUAA 2001</mark>
- Jail ≤ 15 years
- Fine:
    - <mark class="hltr-mygreen2">FINE 5 times</mark> the value of proceeds from unlawful activities or <mark class="hltr-mygreen2">RM5 million</mark>, whichever is higher.

---
# 🎁 10. GIFTS vs BRIBES (VERY TRICKY)

### Key Rule

❗ A gift = bribe IF:

- Given by someone with <mark class="hltr-mygreen2">official relationship</mark>
- Influences decision

---

### IMPORTANT PRINCIPLES

1. Cannot accept gifts from:
    - People you deal with officially

2. Value must be reasonable

---

### VERY COMMON QUESTION

❓ “Can a public officer accept a gift as thanks?”

❌ NO  
✔️ Must give **formal letter instead**

# ⚠️ COMMON MCQ TRAPS

### ❌ Trap 1

“Only public officers can commit corruption”

✔️ WRONG → anyone

---

### ❌ Trap 2

“Bribe must be money”

✔️ WRONG → includes services, discounts, etc.

---

### ❌ Trap 3

“Punishment is jail OR fine”

✔️ WRONG → BOTH

---

### ❌ Trap 4

“Small gift is allowed”

✔️ WRONG → even RM10 can be a bribe

---

### ❌ Trap 5

“Money laundering is separate from corruption”

<mark class="hltr-myyellow">✔️ WRONG</mark> → <mark class="hltr-mygreen2">often charged together</mark>

---

### ❌ Trap 6

“Old corruption cases cannot be prosecuted”

✔️ WRONG → no time limit (from Module 3)
