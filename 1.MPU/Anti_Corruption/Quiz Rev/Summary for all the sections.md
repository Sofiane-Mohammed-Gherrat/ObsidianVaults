### 🏛️ **MACC Act 2009: Legal Summary**

| Section | **On Whom** (Who is targeted?) | **When/Why** (What is the act?) | **Punishment** (If found guilty) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Section 16(a)** | **Anyone** (Public, Private, NGOs, Politicians). | **Soliciting or Accepting** a bribe as an inducement to do (or not do) an official act. Known as **"Passive Bribery"**. | **Imprisonment** $\leq$ 20 years **AND** a **Fine** $\geq$ 5x the bribe value or RM10,000 (whichever is higher). |
| **Section 16(b)** | **Anyone** (Mostly private sector/civilians). | **Giving, Promising, or Offering** a bribe to gain an advantage or avoid punishment. Known as **"Active Bribery"**. | **Imprisonment** $\leq$ 20 years **AND** a **Fine** $\geq$ 5x the bribe value or RM10,000 (whichever is higher). |
| **Section 18** | **Anyone** (Public or Private sectors). | Submitting **False Claims** (receipts/invoices) with the intent to **deceive the employer** (e.g., claiming for goods not supplied). | **Imprisonment** $\leq$ 20 years **AND** a **Fine** $\geq$ 5x the false claim amount or RM10,000 (whichever is higher). |
| **Section 23** | **Public Officers** ONLY. | Using their **official position** to make decisions or take actions where they, a relative, or an associate holds an **interest**. | **Imprisonment** $\leq$ 20 years **AND** a **Fine** $\geq$ 5x the gratification value or RM10,000 (whichever is higher). |

---

### 🔍 **Important Context from Your Lessons**

*   **Why these laws exist:** To prevent the "abuse of entrusted power for private gain" 
*   **The "And" Rule:** For these offenses, the court **must** impose **both** imprisonment and a fine. It is not an "either/or" situation
*   **Collateral Consequences:** 
    *   **Public Servants:** Lose their **pensions** even if jailed for only one day (Pensions Act 1980)
    *   **Company Directors:** Are **disqualified** from being a director (Companies Act 2016)
