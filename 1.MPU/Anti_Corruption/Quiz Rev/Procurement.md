
**Procurement** is essentially the process where a government or organization "goes shopping" to buy goods, services, or construction works from external providers. 🏗️ Think of it as the entire journey from deciding what is needed to picking a vendor and making sure the job gets done!

Here is how it connects to your question:

- **Payment Expedition:** Once a company completes a contract, they submit an invoice to be paid. "Expediting" means someone in authority has the power to speed up that payment process so the company gets its money much faster than usual. 💨
- **Corruption Risk:** Because private companies often have tight budgets and need cash quickly to pay their own workers, the power to "speed up" a check is a massive point of leverage. A dishonest official might hint that a payment will only be "expedited" if they receive a bribe or a "kickback" in return. 🛑
