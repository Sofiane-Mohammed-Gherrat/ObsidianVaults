# 🧠 1. MEANING OF WHITE-COLLAR CRIME (3 KEY ELEMENTS)

A white-collar crime MUST have all 3:

1. ❌ **Non-violent**
2. 💰 **Financially motivated**
3. 👔 **Committed by educated, respected people**
    - People with good positions
    - High social status

👉 MCQ TRAP: **Not about dress colour — that’s blue-collar**

---

# 👕 2. WHITE-COLLAR VS BLUE-COLLAR CRIME

### **White-collar:**

- Non-violent
- Financial motive
- Professionals, executives, managers
- Examples: fraud, insider trading, Ponzi schemes, embezzlement

---

### **Blue-collar:**

- Often violent
- Low-income or uniformed workers
- Why “blue”?  
    ✔️ They wear blue uniforms (factory workers)

---

### Blue-collar crimes include:

- Theft, robbery, housebreaking
- Murder, hurt, kidnapping
- Rape, prostitution

👉 EXAM TRAP: Not all crimes with money are white-collar. Robbery = blue-collar.

---

# 💸 3. FORMS OF WHITE-COLLAR CRIME (MCQ FAVOURITE)

### **Ponzi Scheme**

- Named after <mark class="hltr-myyellow">Charles Ponzi (1919)</mark>
- Pays early investors using money from new investors
- Example: pyramid schemes / MLM scams
- “Get rich quick (illegally)”

# 🧨 5. FAMOUS CASES (MEMORIZE BASICS)

## **ENRON (USA)**

- CEO Kenneth Lay & COO Jeffrey Skilling
- Hid massive debts
- Accounting firm Arthur Andersen <mark class="hltr-myyellow">destroyed documents</mark>
- Share price fell **USD 90 → USD 0.26**
- **28,500** people lost jobs
- Lay died before sentencing; Skilling jailed 12 years
- Andersen lost license

👉 EXAM TRAP: <mark class="hltr-myblue">Don’t mix this with Barings Bank</mark>.

- Used secret **“88888 error account”**
- Hid huge trading losses
- False profits: £28.55 million
- Real losses: **£827 million (US$1.4b)**
- Caused collapse of UK’s oldest merchant bank

# ⚠️ COMMON MCQ TRAPS

1. **“White-collar crimes always involve violence”**  
    ❌ FALSE
    
2. **“Blue-collar crimes are financial crimes”**  
    ❌ FALSE – they are usually violent
    
3. **“Educated people don’t commit white-collar crime”**  
    ❌ FALSE – usually educated professionals
    
4. **“Ponzi schemes pay profit from real investments”**  
    ❌ FALSE – paid using new investors’ money
    
5. **“Macau scam investigated by MACC”**  
    ❌ FALSE – it’s police
    
6. **“Fraudsters are usually young people”**  
    ❌ FALSE – 36–55 age group
    
7. **“Fraud is usually detected through audits”**  
    ❌ FALSE – mostly detected through **reports**
