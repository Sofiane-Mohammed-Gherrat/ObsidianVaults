# 🧠 **2. CAUSES OF CORRUPTION (Academic theories & expert views)**

### ⭐ **Theory 1 — Lord Acton**

- British historian & critic
- Famous principle: **“Power tends to corrupt…”**

- Core points:
    - More power → weaker morality/integrity
    - People in power are more prone to corruption
    - Applies especially to:
        
        - Enforcement officers
        - Procurement officers
        - Licensing authorities

👉 **Focus for MCQs:** Power = central factor driving corruption.

---

### ⭐ **Theory 2 — Abraham Lincoln**

- Power can _change a person_

- Without power → remains good

- With power → may forget roots, abuse authority


👉 **Focus:** Power can corrupt a previously good person.

---

### ⭐ **Theory 3 — Prof. John Hogarth**

- Canadian law professor

- Formula:  
    **GREED + OPPORTUNITY = CORRUPTION**


👉 **Focus:** Two main factors → greed and opportunity.

---

### ⭐ **Theory 4 — “Power + Opportunity – Integrity = Corruption”**

3 core elements:

1. Power

2. Opportunity

3. Lack of integrity


👉 **Focus:** Absence of integrity is essential component.

---

# 🔺 **3. FRAUD TRIANGLE — Causes of Corruption (IMPORTANT)**

Developed by Dr. Donald Cressey (studied 250 fraud criminals).

### The 3 factors:

1. **Pressure**

2. **Opportunity**

3. **Rationalisation**


👉 This is one of the most frequently tested models.

---

## 🔹 Pressure

Examples:

- Financial stress

- Family problems

- Debt

- Lifestyle pressure


---

## 🔹 Opportunity

Example given:

- Complicated procedures → slow approvals

- Customer offers bribe to speed up process


---

## 🔹 Rationalisation

Typical excuses:

- “Everyone does this”

- “I’m only borrowing the money”

- “No one will notice”

- “I deserve this after many years”

- “This is part of doing business”


👉 **MCQ Trap:** Rationalisation = mental justification, NOT motive.

---

# 🚨 **4. SYMPTOMS OF CORRUPTION (MACC’s 7 signs)**

💯 **High MCQ probability — memorize all 7**

### **1. Sudden Lavish Lifestyle**

- Expensive cars, watches, holidays

- Costly renovations

- Luxury items


---

### **2. Cannot Explain Source of Wealth**

**Section 36, MACC Act:**

- S36(1):: MACC can demand property <mark class="hltr-myyellow">declaration</mark>

- S36(2):: Failure to declare = <mark class="hltr-myyellow">offence</mark> (5 years + RM100k fine)

- S36(3):: Failure to <mark class="hltr-myyellow">explain excess wealth</mark> =  **20 years jail + fine (5× value or min RM10,000)**


👉 MCQ loves S36 punishments.

---

### **3. Refuses Promotion or Transfer**

- Wants to stay to protect corrupt income

- Knows loopholes & “clients” who give bribes


---

### **4. Receives Suspicious Phone Calls**

---

### **5. Acting Rich Despite Small Salary**

- Showing off

- Buying branded items

- Paying for colleagues excessively


---

### **6. Suddenly Resigns**

Reasons:

1. Already made enough illegal money

2. Afraid their crimes detected

3. Want to move to new opportunities for corruption


---

### **7. Unusually Close to Customers**

Examples:

- Going on holidays together

- Frequent meals

- Exchanging gifts


Customers = suppliers, contractors.

# ⚠️ COMMON MCQ TRAPS

1. **“Corruption requires violence.”**  
    ❌ No — corruption is non-violent.
    
2. **“Rationalisation is a financial pressure.”**  
    ❌ No — it’s mental justification.
    
3. **“MACC needs a warrant to arrest corruption offenders.”**  
    ❌ No — seizable offence.
    
4. **“Only rich people show symptom #1.”**  
    ❌ Wrong — it’s about sudden unexplained lifestyle jump.
    
5. **“Only completed cases appear in database.”**  
    ✅ True — only convicted cases.
    
6. **“CPI measures actual corruption levels.”**  
    ❌ It measures **perception**, not actual corruption.
