# Module 7 – Report Corruption, Preventive Measures & Government Initiatives (MCQ Revision)

## 1️⃣ Legal Duty to Report Corruption

### Key idea

Reporting corruption is **both a moral duty and a legal duty**.

### MACC Act 2009 – Section 25

#### Receiver of gratification

**Section 25(1)**  
?
Anyone <mark class="hltr-myyellow">who receives gratification (bribe)</mark> must <mark class="hltr-mygreen2">report</mark> to:
- **MACC**
- **Police**


**Failure to report – Section 25(2)**

Punishment:

- Fine **≤ RM100,000**

- Imprisonment **≤ 10 years**

- Or both


---

#### Giver of gratification

**Section 25(3)**  
Anyone **who gives gratification** must also report it.

**Failure to report – Section 25(4)**

Punishment:

- Fine **≤ RM10,000**

- Imprisonment **≤ 2 years**

- Or both


---

### Reporting channels

Reports can be made through:
?
- MACC offices
- Police
- Various reporting channels


#### Poison-pen letters

Anonymous reports are accepted but **not encouraged** because:

- Often **insufficient information**

- Investigators **cannot contact informant**


Best method:  
👉 Report **directly to MACC**

---

### Confidentiality of informant

Public should not fear reporting because:

- Identity **kept confidential**

# 3️⃣ Protection of Reports & Whistleblowers

Protection is provided under:
?
1️⃣ **Section 65 MACC Act 2009**  
2️⃣ **Whistleblower Protection Act 2010**

---

## Section 65 MACC Act 2009

Provides **confidentiality protection**:

1️⃣ Information received in report **kept confidential** until accused is charged.  
2️⃣ Identity of complainant **cannot be disclosed in court**.

Note:  
Even the complainant **cannot get a copy of their own report**.

---

# 4️⃣ Whistleblower Protection

### Definition

A **whistleblower** is:

> Someone with **indirect knowledge** of corruption and **not involved in the act**.

---

## Whistleblower Protection Act 2010 (WPA 2010)

Purpose:

- Protect individuals who report **improper conduct** in:
    - public sector
    - private sector

Malaysia introduced this law to comply with the **United Nations Convention Against Corruption (UNCAC)**.

Malaysia:

- Signed: **9 Dec 2003**
- Ratified: **24 Sept 2008**

---

## 3 Forms of Protection (Sections 7–10 WPA 2010)

1️⃣ **Protection of confidential information**

2️⃣ **Immunity from**

- civil action    
- criminal action
- disciplinary action

3️⃣ **Protection against detrimental action**

---

### Important condition

Whistleblower must:

✅ Be **willing to reveal identity to MACC**

Anonymous reports:  
❌ **No protection provided** because identity unknown.

---

## Confidential Information Protection

Confidential information includes:

- Identity
- Occupation
- Address
- Location
- Information disclosed by whistleblower

### Punishment for disclosure

- Fine **≤ RM50,000**
- Imprisonment **≤ 10 years**
- Or both

---

## Detrimental Action (against whistleblower)

Examples:

- Injury or damage
- Intimidation
- Harassment
- Employment interference:
    
    - dismissal
	
    - demotion
    
    - suspension
    
    - job transfer
    
    - termination

### Punishment

- Fine **≤ RM100,000**
- Imprisonment **≤ 15 years**
- Or both

---

# 5️⃣ Witness Protection

### Witness definition

A **witness** is someone who has **direct knowledge** of corruption (through seeing, hearing, or involvement).

---

## Witness Protection Act 2009

Purpose:

- Protect witnesses **before, during, and after trial**.

Protection handled by:  
👉 **Witness Protection Unit**  
<mark class="hltr-myyellow">(under Prime Minister’s Department)</mark>

---

### Process

1️⃣ Witness reports threat to MACC  
2️⃣ MACC applies to Witness Protection Unit

---

### Examples of protection

Depending on seriousness:

Example protections:

- Police patrol around house
- Approval for **tinted car windows**

---

# 6️⃣ Tips to Avoid Getting Involved in Corruption (8 Tips)

### Tip 1

Never start accepting bribes because:

- It leads to **bigger demands later**.

---

### Tip 2

Be careful of **insincere compliments**.

---

### Tip 3

Do not share personal problems with:

- contractors
- suppliers

They may see it as **a hint for bribery**.

---

### Tip 4

Decline entertainment such as:

- luxury hotel entertainment
- golf trips
- holiday packages

---

### Tip 5

Maintain **professional relationships only** with contractors/suppliers.

Avoid:

- holidays together
- personal sharing
- socialising too much.

---

### Tip 6

If someone attempts to bribe you:

1. Reject firmly

2. Show strong principles

3. Warn them not to repeat

4. Report to MACC if they persist.


---

### Tip 7

Do not share personal matters with contractors.

Only discuss **official matters**.

---

### Tip 8

Choose friends wisely.

Bad friends may **influence you into corruption**.

---

# 7️⃣ Government Anti-Corruption Initiatives

### 1️⃣ National Anti-Corruption Plan (NACP)

- Period: **2019–2023**

- Launched: **29 Jan 2019**

---

### 2️⃣ National Anti-Corruption Strategy (NACS)

- Period: **2024–2028**

- Launched: **7 May 2024**

- **Most recent anti-corruption policy**


---

### Relationship

NACS **continues** the NACP.

---

### International commitment

Both plans support Malaysia's obligations under:

**United Nations Convention Against Corruption (UNCAC)**.

Requirement:  
Each country must have **documented anti-corruption policies**.

    - Section **25(1)** – receiver must report

---

### Memorise punishments

Very common MCQ questions.

Receiver failure to report:

- **RM100,000**
- **10 years prison**


Giver failure to report:

- **RM10,000**
- **2 years prison**


Confidential info disclosure:

- **RM50,000**
- **10 years prison**


Detrimental action:

- **RM100,000**
- **15 years prison**

---

### Know differences

Whistleblower vs Witness

|Whistleblower|Witness|
|---|---|
|Indirect knowledge|Direct knowledge|
|WPA 2010|Witness Protection Act 2009|
|Not involved in act|May have seen/heard act|

- **8 tips to avoid corruption**
