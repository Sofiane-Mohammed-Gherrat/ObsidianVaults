## 1. Case Overview and Facts

- **The Core Issue**: The case centered on the misappropriation of **RM42 million** from SRC International Sdn Bhd (a former 1MDB subsidiary) into Najib Razak’s personal bank accounts between late 2014 and early 2015.
    
- **Conflict of Interest**: The situation was exacerbated by Najib simultaneously holding the roles of **Prime Minister and Finance Minister**, which removed essential institutional checks and balances.
    
- **Timeline**: Investigations led to <mark class="hltr-mypurple">charges</mark> in <mark class="hltr-mypurple">July 2018</mark>, followed by a **trial** that began in <mark class="hltr-mypurple">April 2019</mark> and involved 57 witnesses over 58 days.
    

## 2. Legal Findings and Sentencing

Najib Razak was convicted on <mark class="hltr-myyellow">7 charges</mark> across three categories:

### The 7 Charges Breakdown

#### 1. Abuse of Power (1 Charge)

- **The Law:** Section 23 of the MACC Act 2009.
    
- **The Act:** Using his position as Prime Minister and Finance Minister to approve a RM4 billion loan from the Retirement Fund (KWAP) to SRC, in exchange for a **RM42 million bribe/gratification**.
    

#### 2. Criminal Breach of Trust / CBT (3 Charges)

- **The Law:** **<u>Section 409</u>** of the **Penal Code**.
    
- **The Act:** Misappropriating funds he was entrusted with as a public servant. These are split by the specific transfers into his accounts:
    
    - **Charge 1:** Misappropriating **RM27 million**.
    - **Charge 2:** Misappropriating **RM5 million**.
    - **Charge 3:** Misappropriating **RM10 million**.

#### 3. Money Laundering (3 Charges)

- **The Law:** **<u>Section 4(1)(b)</u>** of the **<u>AMLATFAPUAA Act 2001</u>**.

- **The Act:** Receiving and dealing with the proceeds of illegal activities. Split by the transfer amounts:
	
    - **Charge 1:** Receiving **RM27 million**.
    - **Charge 2:** Receiving **RM5 million**.
    - **Charge 3:** Receiving **RM10 million**.

## 3. Impact Analysis

- **Public and Political**: The scandal severely damaged public trust in government institutions and was labeled a **"national embarrassment"** by the appellate court.

- **Economic**: Beyond the RM42 million, the case increased national borrowing risks and diminished foreign investor confidence.

- **Institutional**: The case exposed deep-seated flaws in the oversight of Government-Linked Companies (GLCs), where political interference can render auditing and approval processes mere formalities.


## 4. Key Lessons and Preventive Measures

The report suggests several strategies to prevent future occurrences:

- **Separation of Power**: Limit the concentration of power and implement stricter conflict-of-interest disclosure rules.

- **Financial Transparency**: Upgrade real-time monitoring and "red flag" systems for **politically exposed persons (PEPs)**.

- **Independent Oversight**: Ensure that boards of public-linked entities are truly independent and protected from political capture.

- **Judicial Independence**: Protect the autonomy of investigators and prosecutors to ensure that elite status does not lead to impunity.


## 5. Conclusion

The document concludes that while the three-tier judicial process successfully demonstrated that **no one is above the law**, the length and complexity of the trial highlight that **prevention** through robust institutional reform is far more effective than prosecution after the fact.
