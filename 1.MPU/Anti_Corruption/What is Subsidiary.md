
**SRC International was a subsidiary of 1MDB**, it means that **SRC International was originally a company owned and controlled by the 1MDB fund**. To understand this clearly, we need to explain what a **subsidiary** is and how it applied in this case.

---

# 1. What a “Subsidiary” Means

>In business, a **subsidiary** is a company that is **owned or controlled by another company**, called the **parent company**.

Usually this means the parent company owns **more than 50% of the shares** and therefore controls:

- the board of directors
- major financial decisions
- strategic direction.

So if **Company A owns Company B**, then **Company B is a subsidiary of Company A**.

---

# 2. How This Applied to SRC International

The state investment fund **1Malaysia Development Berhad (1MDB)** created **SRC International** in **January 2011** as one of its companies. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1MDB?utm_source=chatgpt.com "1MDB"))

The purpose of SRC International was to:

- invest in **energy resources**
- invest in **natural resources and minerals**
- help secure long-term strategic resources for Malaysia. ([CNA](https://www.todayonline.com/world/asia/mystery-src-international-millions-transferred-najibs-accounts?utm_source=chatgpt.com "Mystery of SRC International, millions transferred into Najib’s accounts - TODAY"))

Because **1MDB created and owned it**, SRC International was considered a **subsidiary of 1MDB**.

---

# 3. What Happened Later (Important Detail)

In **February 2012**, the ownership of SRC International was **transferred from 1MDB to Malaysia’s Ministry of Finance (MOF Inc)**. ([Malay Mail](https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2015/06/10/former-1mdb-subsidiary-had-no-extra-debt-since-last-audit-parliament-told/912819?utm_source=chatgpt.com "Former 1MDB subsidiary had no extra debt since last audit, Parliament told | Malay Mail"))

This means:

- SRC **stopped being a subsidiary of 1MDB**
- It became directly owned by the **government through the Ministry of Finance**.

However, SRC’s **early funding and structure were still connected to 1MDB**, which is why it is often discussed as part of the broader 1MDB scandal.

---

# 4. Why This Relationship Matters in the Case

The subsidiary relationship matters because:

1. **1MDB created SRC International**
2. SRC later received **RM4 billion in government-guaranteed loans**
3. Part of that money was later traced to **Najib Razak’s personal bank accounts**
4. This became the basis of the **SRC corruption trial**

So even though SRC was later moved out of 1MDB, **its origins and financial links connected it to the larger scandal**.
