# 1. Why Writing Is Strategic

At the **university level**, writing is not just about grammar. It focuses on:

- communicating **ideas clearly**

- communicating **logically**

- communicating **persuasively**


Professional writing can influence:

- decisions
    
- funding approvals
    
- policies
    
- reputations
    

Your writing reflects:

- your **thinking ability**
    
- your **professionalism**
    

Unclear writing may cause:

- financial loss
    
- conflict
    
- misinterpretation.
    

### MCQ Focus

- Purpose of professional writing
    
- Consequences of poor writing

1. Not identifying the **target audience**
    
2. Using **complex vocabulary** to sound academic
    
3. **Poor paragraph structure**
    
4. **Wordy sentences**
    
5. **Repeating ideas without development**

# 3. What Is Rhetoric

Definition:

**Rhetoric = the art of using language effectively and persuasively.**

Key points:

- originated in **classical Greek philosophy**
    
- helps writers **influence readers logically and ethically**
    
- helps the audience **understand and trust the message**.
    

### MCQ Focus

Definition of rhetoric.

---

# 4. The Rhetorical Triangle

The rhetorical triangle has **three elements**:

|Element|Focus|
|---|---|
|Ethos|credibility|
|Logos|logic|
|Pathos|emotion|

Effective writing **balances all three**.

---

# 5. Ethos (Credibility)

Ethos refers to:

- **trustworthiness**
    
- **authority of the writer**
    

Ways to build ethos:

- using reliable sources
    
- accurate information
    
- professional tone
    
- admitting limitations honestly
    

Example:

> “As a doctor with 20 years of experience…”

### MCQ Keyword

**Ethos = trust / credibility**

---

# 6. Logos (Logical Appeal)

Logos focuses on:

- reasoning
    
- logical structure
    
- evidence
    

Strong logos includes:

- statistics
    
- research findings
    
- factual comparisons
    
- cause-and-effect relationships.
    

Example:

> “Studies show that 8 out of 10 students improve after practice.”

### MCQ Keyword

**Logos = logic / evidence**

---

# 7. Pathos (Emotional Appeal)

Pathos appeals to:

- emotions
    
- values
    

Important:

- must be **ethical**
    
- should not be **manipulative**
    

Often used to highlight:

- human impact
    
- personal experiences
    

Example:

> “Imagine a child going to bed hungry every night.”

### MCQ Keyword

**Pathos = feelings / emotions**

---

# 8. Structure of Writing

Professional documents follow a **three-part structure**:

### 1. Introduction

Functions:

- introduce topic
    
- provide background
    
- present **thesis statement**
    
- show direction of discussion
    

### 2. Body

Functions:

- develop arguments
    
- each paragraph = **one main idea**
    
- provide evidence
    
- use transitions
    

### 3. Conclusion

Functions:

- summarise main points
    
- restate importance
    
- give recommendations
    
- leave strong final impression.
    

---

# 9. Why Structure Is Important

Structure:

- guides readers step-by-step
    
- prevents confusion
    
- improves readability
    
- helps readers who **skim documents**.
    

---

# 10. Paragraph Structure

A good paragraph contains:

1. **Topic sentence** – main idea
    
2. **Supporting sentences** – explanation
    
3. **Evidence** – proof or examples
    
4. **Concluding sentence** – links to next paragraph.
    

### MCQ Focus

> A paragraph begins with a **topic sentence**.

# 11. Communication Process

Communication includes:

1. Sender
    
2. Message
    
3. Channel
    
4. Receiver
    
5. Feedback
    

Feedback:

- confirms whether the message was understood.
    

---

# 12. Noise in Communication

Noise = anything that interferes with communication.

Types:

|Type|Meaning|
|---|---|
|Physical|environmental distractions|
|Psychological|stress, bias, assumptions|
|Cultural|different norms|
|Language|vocabulary barriers|

---

# 13. Cross-Cultural Communication

Key ideas:

- misunderstandings are common in multicultural environments
    
- tone may be interpreted differently
    
- direct communication may appear rude
    
- professional writing must be **culturally sensitive**.
    

---

# 14. The Four Cs of Writing

Important concept for exams.

|C|Meaning|
|---|---|
|Clarity|clear ideas|
|Continuity|smooth flow|
|Concision|no unnecessary words|
|Coherence|logical organisation|

---

# 15. Clarity

To improve clarity:

- use **specific vocabulary**
    
- avoid vague pronouns
    
- use **active voice**
    
- keep **subject and verb close**.
    

---

# 16. Concision

Concision means:

- writing **briefly without losing meaning**
    

Techniques:

- remove redundant expressions
    
- remove filler words
    
- shorten long phrases
    

Example:

> “Due to the fact that” → “Because”.

---

# 17. Continuity

Continuity means:

- smooth connection between sentences.
    

Methods:

- transition words
    
    - however
        
    - therefore
        
    - in addition
        
- consistent tense
    
- consistent subject
    

---

# 18. Coherence

Coherence ensures:

- ideas are logically organised
    
- paragraphs support the thesis
    
- the text flows smoothly
    

Methods:

- headings and subheadings
    
- logical structure
    
- reading aloud to check flow.
    

---

# 19. Audience Analysis

Different audiences require different writing styles.

Examples:

|Audience|Preference|
|---|---|
|Decision makers|concise summaries|
|Technical experts|detailed explanations|
|General readers|simple language|

---

# 20. Types of Readers

Three types:

|Reader|Role|
|---|---|
|Primary|decision maker|
|Secondary|advisors or evaluators|
|Tertiary|indirect audience|

---

# 21. WHO – WHY – HOW Model

Used for **audience analysis**.

### WHO (Demographics)

- age
    
- education
    
- occupation
    
- culture
    
- socioeconomic background
    

### WHY (Psychographics)

- beliefs
    
- values
    
- attitudes
    
- goals
    
- lifestyle
    

### HOW (Situational factors)

- digital or printed document
    
- time available
    
- emotional state
    
- expertise level.
    

---

# 22. Building Goodwill in Writing

Ways to maintain good relationships with readers:

- use respectful language
    
- use neutral tone
    
- avoid blaming
    
- acknowledge concerns
    
- offer constructive solutions.
