## For each one of the situation:

#### 1️⃣ Identify Audience Type

• Who is the primary audience?
• Who is the secondary audience?
• Who is the tertiary audience?

#### 2️⃣ WHO (Demographics)

• Age?
• Gender?
• Education level?
• Cultural background?
• Income level?

#### 3️⃣ WHY (Psychographics)

• What do they care about?
• What are their values?
• What motivates them?
• Possible resistance?

#### 4️⃣ HOW (Situational Analysis)

• Where will they read it? (phone? email? printed memo?)
• Reading skill level?
• Time available?
• Formal or informal tone?

## 3. Marketing & External Outreach

_Focusing on: Health Poster (Elderly), Social Media (Teens), & Website Content (Working Adults)._

## **Health Awareness Poster (Elderly Citizens)**

- **WHO:** Ages 65+; Retired; Diverse income; High school to University level.
    
- **WHY:** Value longevity and family; Motivated by health/independence; Resistant to complex tech/jargon.
    
- **HOW:** Printed posters in clinics/centers; Large font; Easy reading; **Friendly & Encouraging.**


## **Social Media Content (Road Safety for Teens)**

- **WHO:** Ages 13–19; Students; Tech-savvy; Gen Z/Alpha.
    
- **WHY:** Value social status and freedom; Motivated by peer trends; Resistant to "preaching" or authority.
    
- **HOW:** Phone (TikTok/IG); Skimming speed; 5–10 seconds; **Casual & Visual.**


## **Website Content (Course for Working Adults)**

- **WHO:** Ages 25–45; Working professionals; Degree holders; Mid-income.
    
- **WHY:** Value career advancement/salary; Motivated by convenience; Resistant to time-wasters.
    
- **HOW:** Desktop/Mobile; High reading level; 3 mins; **Persuasive & Goal-oriented.**

| **WHO**           | 18–70; General Public; Users of the service.                       | 40–60 (Professors); High Education; Institutional mindset.                             | 25–50; High Education; Media-savvy.                                            |
| **WHY**           | Values reliability; Motivated by compensation; **Very Resistant.** | Values merit/integrity; Motivated by equity; Resistant to "sob stories" without proof. | Values "the scoop"; Motivated by community impact; Resistant to boring news.   |
