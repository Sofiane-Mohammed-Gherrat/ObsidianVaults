# 1. Purpose of the Lecture

The lecture teaches **professional email communication in the workplace**.

Students learn to:

- Structure professional emails

- Use proper **tone and etiquette**

- Communicate clearly with:
	
    - colleagues
    - lecturers

# 2. Why Email Communication is Important

Email is **the most common workplace communication tool**.

Professionals use email for:

- Communicating with colleagues

- Contacting clients/customers

- Sending reports or documents

- Requesting information

- Scheduling meetings


### Problems caused by poor email communication

- Misunderstandings

- Project delays

- Damage to professional reputation


⚠️ **MCQ focus:** consequences of poor email communication.

# 3. Informal vs Professional Emails

### Informal Emails

Used with:

- Friends
- Family


Characteristics:

- Slang
- Emojis 👀
- Abbreviations
- Casual tone

---

### Professional Emails

Used in:

- Workplace
- Academic settings

Characteristics:

- Clear structure
- Polite tone
- Professional language
- Complete sentences
- No slang or emojis

⚠️ **MCQ focus:** difference between informal and professional emails.

# 4. The 5 Cs of Effective Emails

Professional emails follow the **5 Cs**:

1. **Clear** – easy to understand
2. **Concise** – not unnecessarily long
3. **Courteous** – polite and respectful
4. **Correct** – accurate grammar and facts
5. **Complete** – all information included


⚠️ **Very likely MCQ question.**

---

# 5. Structure of a Professional Email

A professional email normally includes:

1. **Subject line**
2. **Greeting / Salutation**
3. **Opening sentence**
4. **Email body**
5. **Closing sentence**
6. **Sign-off**
7. **Signature**

⚠️ **Memorize this order.**

---

# 6. Subject Line

The **subject line is the first thing the reader sees**.

A good subject line should be:

- Clear
- Specific
- Short

### Good examples

- Request for Internship Information
- Meeting Confirmation – 15 May
- Request for Assignment Extension
- Submission of Project Proposal

### Poor example

- Subject: Hi


⚠️ **MCQ focus:** characteristics of good subject lines.

---

# 7. Professional Greetings

Common greetings:

- Dear Mr. Lee
- Dear Dr. Tan
- Dear Professor Lim
- Dear Hiring Manager

### Avoid:

- Hey
- Hi bro
- Hello boss

⚠️ **MCQ focus:** identifying appropriate greetings.

---

# 8. Opening Sentence

The opening sentence explains **why you are writing**.

Examples:

- I am writing to enquire about…
- I would like to request…
- Thank you for your email regarding…
- I am writing to inform you that…

---

# 9. Email Body

The body explains the **main message**.

Guidelines:

- Provide necessary information
- Use short paragraphs
- <mark class="hltr-mygreen2">One idea per paragraph</mark>
- <mark class="hltr-myyellow">Avoid unnecessary details</mark>

---

# 10. Professional Tone

Professional tone must be:

- Respectful
- Neutral
- Polite

### Example

Unprofessional:

> You didn’t send the report.

Professional:

> I noticed that the report has not yet been received.

---

### Polite expressions

Instead of:

> Send me the report.

Write:

> Could you please send the report?

Instead of:

> You made a mistake.

Write:

> There may be a small error in the document.

⚠️ **MCQ focus:** identifying polite language.

---

# 11. Closing Sentence

Purpose:

- Signals the end of the email
- <mark class="hltr-mygreen2">Shows expectation</mark>


Examples:

- I look forward to your response.

- Thank you for your assistance.

- Please let me know if you require further information.


---

# 12. Professional Sign-Off

Common sign-offs:

- Kind regards

- Best regards

- Sincerely

- Thank you


Avoid:

- Cheers

- Bye

- Thanks bro


⚠️ **MCQ focus:** appropriate professional closing.

# 📝 From Gemini 


## **1. The Core Principles: The 5 Cs**

Effective professional emails must follow these five criteria:

- **Clear:** The message is easy for the reader to understand.

- **Concise:** The email is short and avoids unnecessary length.

- **Courteous:** The tone is polite, respectful, and neutral.

- **Correct:** Facts are accurate, and there are no grammar or spelling errors.

- **Complete:** All necessary information and attachments are included.


## **2. Structure of a Professional Email**

For an MCQ, you should know the correct order and purpose of each element:

1. **Subject Line:** Summarizes the purpose (e.g., "Request for Meeting").

2. **Greeting/Salutation:** Politely addresses the reader (e.g., "Dear Dr. Tan").

3. **Opening Sentence:** Immediately explains the reason for writing.

4. **Email Body:** Clearly explains the main message in short paragraphs.

5. **Closing Sentence:** Signals the end and indicates next steps or appreciation.

6. **Sign-off:** A professional closing phrase (e.g., "Kind regards").

7. **Signature:** Includes your name and contact details.


## **3. Writing Techniques to Focus On**

- **Subject Lines:** Must be short, specific, and clear. Avoid generic subjects like "Hi".
    
- **Tone Shifts:** Be able to identify professional vs. unprofessional language:
    
    - _Unprofessional:_ "You made a mistake" or "Send me the report".
    
    - _Professional:_ "There may be a small error" or "Could you please send the report?".
    
- **Greetings and Closings:** Stick to formal options like "Dear Mr./Dr./Prof." and "Sincerely" or "Best regards". Avoid "Hey," "Hi bro," "Cheers," or "Bye".
    
- **Paragraphing:** Use short paragraphs and focus on **one idea** per paragraph.


## **4. Professional Etiquette and Behavior**

- **Avoid:** Writing in ALL CAPS, using emojis, slang, or informal abbreviations.

- **Best Practices:** Respond promptly, check your grammar/spelling, and verify that all attachments are correct before sending.

- **When NOT to use Email:** Avoid email for **urgent matters**, **complex discussions**, or **emotional conflicts**. Use phone calls or meetings instead.


## **Quiz Tips:**

- **Scenario-based Questions:** You may be asked to choose the "best" subject line or opening sentence for a specific situation, like asking for an internship or a deadline extension.

- **Error Identification:** Watch for "red flags" such as missing signatures, informal language, or unclear subject lines.

- **Consequences:** Remember that poor email communication leads to misunderstandings, project delays, and damage to your reputation.
