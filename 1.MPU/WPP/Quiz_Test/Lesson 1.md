# 1. Why Writing Is Strategic

Key idea: Writing is **not just grammar**, it is about **clear, logical, persuasive communication**.

Important points:

- Writing communicates **ideas clearly and logically**.
- Professional writing influences:
	
    - decisions
    - funding approvals
    - policies
    - reputations
    
- Writing reflects:
    
    - **thinking ability**
    - **professionalism**
    
- Poor writing can cause:
    
    - financial loss
    - conflict
    - misunderstanding.

# 4. The Rhetorical Triangle

Very likely MCQ.

Three elements:

|Element|Focus|Meaning|
|---|---|---|
|**Ethos**|Credibility|Trustworthiness of writer|
|**Logos**|Logic|Facts, reasoning, evidence|
|**Pathos**|Emotion|Appeal to feelings|

#### Ethos:
> In professional writing, ==admiting limitations== or mistakes honestly and ==demonstrating accountability== <mark class="hltr-myyellow">strengthens the writer's ethos</mark>, as it shows integrity and a commitment to professional standards.

**Key concept:**  
Effective writing **balances all three**.

# 5. Ethos (Credibility)

Definition:

> Trustworthiness and authority of the writer.

How to build Ethos:

- reliable sources
- accurate information
- professional tone
- honest acknowledgement of limitations

**Keyword for MCQ:**  

<mark class="hltr-myyellow">Ethos = Trust / Credibility</mark>

# 6. Logos (Logical Appeal)

Definition:

> Persuasion using **logic and evidence**

Methods:

- data
- statistics
- research
- examples
- cause-effect reasoning

**Keyword:**  
**Logos = Facts / Logic**

---

# 7. Pathos (Emotional Appeal)

Definition:

> Appeal to **values and emotions**

Important rule:

- should be **ethical, not manipulative**

Used to highlight:

- human impact
- emotional consequences

**Keyword:**  

<mark class="hltr-myyellow">Pathos = Emotion / Feelings</mark>

# 8. Structure of Writing

Professional documents follow **three-part structure**.

### 1. Introduction

Functions:

- introduce topic
- give background
- present **thesis statement**
- show direction of discussion


### 2. Body

Functions:

- develop arguments
- <mark class="hltr-mygreen2">one main idea per paragraph</mark>
- provide evidence
- use transitions


### 3. Conclusion

Functions:

- summarise key points
- restate importance
- give recommendations
- leave strong final impression

---

# 9. Paragraph Structure

A good paragraph has:

1. **Topic sentence** → main idea
2. **Supporting sentences** → explanation
3. **Evidence/examples**
4. **Concluding sentence** → link to next paragraph

**MCQ possibility:**  
“What should a paragraph begin with?”  
Answer → **Topic sentence**

# 13. The Four Cs of Writing

Very important for MCQ.

|C|Meaning|
|---|---|
|**Clarity**|clear and precise ideas|
|**Continuity**|smooth flow between sentences|
|**Concision**|remove unnecessary words|
|**Coherence**|logical organisation|

---

# 14. Clarity

Ways to achieve clarity:

- use **specific vocabulary**

- avoid vague pronouns (this, it)

- prefer **active voice**

- keep **subject and verb close**

---

# 15. Concision

Meaning:

- **short but meaningful writing**


Techniques:

- remove redundant phrases

- remove filler words

- replace long phrases with short words


Example:

- “Due to the fact that” → **Because**


---

# 16. Continuity

Focus:

- smooth connection between sentences.


Methods:

- transition words:
    
    - however
    - therefore
    - in addition
    
- consistent tense and subject

- important info at sentence end


---

# 17. Coherence

Meaning:

- ideas flow logically.

Ways to achieve:

- paragraphs support thesis

- logical organisation

- headings/subheadings

- smooth reading flow


---

# 18. Audience Analysis

Important idea:

**Different audiences need different writing styles.**

Examples:

- decision makers → concise <mark class="hltr-myyellow">summaries</mark>

- technical experts → detailed <mark class="hltr-mygreen2">explanations</mark>

- general readers → <mark class="hltr-myblue">simple language</mark>

# 19. Three Types of Readers

|Reader|Role|
|---|---|
|**Primary**|decision maker|
|**Secondary**|advisors/evaluators|
|**Tertiary**|indirectly affected audience|
secondary = defined as advisors or evaluators who <mark class="hltr-myyellow">review the document</mark> to <mark class="hltr-mygreen2">provide expertise</mark> or ensure compliance, whereas the primary reader is the actual decision-maker.

> **Tertiary readers** =>  ❌ not the direct recipients of the message but <mark class="hltr-mygreen2">are indirectly affected</mark> by it or might <mark class="hltr-myyellow">use it for future reference</mark>.

# 20. Audience Analysis Model

Remember **WHO – WHY – HOW**

### WHO (Demographics)

> focuses on the ==identifiable characteristics== of your readers


- age
- education
- occupation
- cultural background
- socioeconomic status

### WHY (Psychographics)

> focuses on the ==internal motivations== and personality of the reader

- beliefs
- values
- attitudes
- lifestyle
- goals

### HOW (Situational Factors)

>  focuses on the ==physical and environmental context== of the reader

- digital or printed document
- time available
- emotional state
- expertise level

# 21. Building Goodwill in Writing

Important for professional tone.

> Goodwill =  ==respectful==, ==neutral language==, acknowledging the ==reader's concerns==, and focusing on ==constructive solutions== rather than adopting a ==blaming== or aggressive tone.

Ways to build goodwill:

- respectful language

- neutral tone

- avoid blaming

- acknowledge concerns

- offer solutions
