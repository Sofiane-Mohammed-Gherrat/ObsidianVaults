# CV And Cover Letter 

---
# 1. Why Job Documents Matter

Key facts often tested in MCQs:

- Recruitment is **highly competitive**.
    
- Recruiters typically:
    
    - Spend **6–10 seconds scanning a CV**.
        
    - **Eliminate over 70% of applicants** during the first scan.


Good job documents must:

- Communicate **information quickly and clearly**
    
- Demonstrate **professionalism**
    
- Highlight **achievements and competencies**
    
- Show **relevance to the job**
    

⚠️ **Focus:** numbers and reasons for rejection.

---

# 2. Curriculum Vitae (CV)

### Definition

A **CV (Curriculum Vitae)** means <mark class="hltr-myyellow">“course of life.”</mark>

It is a **detailed document** describing:

- Academic history
    
- Research experience
    
- Publications
    
- Professional experience
    
- Achievements
    
- Skills and competencies
    

### Characteristics

- **Long and comprehensive**
    
- Can be **several pages**
    
- Academic focus
    

### Used for

- Academic careers
    
- Research positions
    
- Scholarships
    
- Postgraduate programmes
    
- Fellowships
    

⚠️ **Focus:**  
CV = **long, academic, detailed**.

---

# 3. Resume

### Definition

A **resume** is a **short professional summary** of qualifications.

### Characteristics

- **1–2 pages**
    
- Focuses on **relevant work experience**
    
- **Tailored for a specific job**
    
- Highlights **achievements**
    

### Used for

- Corporate jobs
    
- Private sector
    
- Business environments
    
- Entry-level professional jobs
    

⚠️ **Focus:**  
Resume = **short + job-specific**.

---

# 4. CV vs Resume (VERY likely MCQ)

|CV|Resume|
|---|---|
|Longer|Short (1–2 pages)|
|Academic record|Work experience focused|
|Includes research & publications|Focuses on job achievements|
|Used in academia/research|Used in corporate/business|

⚠️ **Memorise this comparison.**

---

# 5. Cover Letter

### Definition

A **short document sent with a CV or resume**.

### Purpose

- Introduce the candidate
    
- Explain **interest in the job**
    
- Highlight **key strengths**
    
- Encourage employer to **read the CV**
    

### Key Feature

- **Persuasive**
    
- **Tailored to the organisation**
    

⚠️ **Focus:**  
Cover letter = **introduction + motivation**.

---

# 6. Application Letter

### Definition

A **formal request for employment**.

### Contents

- Qualifications
    
- Relevant experience
    
- Reasons candidate is suitable
    
- Request for job consideration
    

⚠️ **Focus:**  
Application letter = **more detailed job request**.

---

# 7. Cover Letter vs Application Letter

| Cover Letter                 | Application Letter      |
| ---------------------------- | ----------------------- |
| Accompanies CV               | Formal job request      |
| Short                        | Longer and detailed     |
| Focus on interest/motivation | Explains qualifications |
| Encourages reading CV        | Can be main application |

⚠️ **Common MCQ question.**

---

# 8. Structure of an Application Letter

You should remember the **order**:

1. Sender's address

2. Date

3. Employer's address
    
4. Salutation
    
5. Opening paragraph
    
6. Body paragraphs
    
7. Closing paragraph
    
8. Complimentary close
    
9. Signature
    

⚠️ **Memorise sequence.**

---

# 9. Opening Paragraph Strategy

Should include:

- The **position applied for**
    
- **Where the job was advertised**
    
- Brief **introduction of the candidate**
    

Purpose:  
➡️ Encourage employer to keep reading.

---

# 10. Body Paragraph Strategy

Provide **evidence of suitability**.

Include:

- Qualifications
    
- Skills with examples
    
- Achievements
    
- Link to job requirements
    

Example concept:

Weak:

> I have good communication skills.

Strong:

> Delivered presentations to 40 staff members.

⚠️ **Employers prefer evidence, not general claims.**

---

# 11. Closing Paragraph Strategy

Must:

- Reaffirm interest
    
- Request interview opportunity
    
- Provide contact details
    
- Thank the employer
    

Tone:  
**Professional and confident**

---

# 12. Complimentary Close

Rules:

If name is known:

- **Yours sincerely**
    

If name is unknown:

- **Yours faithfully**
    

Other acceptable:

- Kind regards
    
- Best regards
    

⚠️ Avoid **informal endings**.

---

# 13. Academic CV Structure

Typical sections:

- Contact information
    
- Academic qualifications
    
- Research experience
    
- Teaching experience
    
- Publications
    
- Conference presentations
    
- Professional experience
    
- Skills
    
- Awards
    
- References
    

⚠️ Focus: **research + academic contributions**.

---

# 14. Professional CV Structure

Typical sections:

- Contact information
    
- Professional summary
    
- Work experience
    
- Education
    
- Key skills
    
- Certifications
    
- Achievements
    
- References
    

⚠️ Focus: **work experience + skills**.

---

# 15. Powerful CV Writing Strategies

Strong CVs include:

- Clear structure
    
- Logical organisation
    
- **Quantified achievements**
    
- Professional formatting
    
- **Strong action verbs**
    

Examples of action verbs:

- Developed
    
- Managed
    
- Implemented
    
- Coordinated
    
- Designed
    
- Led
    

⚠️ Action verbs show **competence and leadership**.

---

# 16. Common CV Mistakes

Very testable list:

- Spelling or grammar errors
    
- Poor formatting
    
- Too much personal information
    
- Long paragraphs instead of bullet points
    
- No measurable achievements
    
- Irrelevant experience
    

⚠️ These reduce professionalism.

---

# 17. Key Concept (Final Idea)

Job documents are **persuasive communication tools**.

They:

- Shape employer perception
    
- Demonstrate professionalism
    
- Highlight strengths
    
- Differentiate candidates

# 🧩 Quiz

In the context of professional job documents, what is the specific rhetorical purpose of an Application Letter compared to a Cover Letter?
?
The Application Letter is a more formal request that explains qualifications in greater detail to argue for suitability.
> While often used interchangeably, an Application Letter is typically a more formal and detailed request for employment that acts as a persuasive argument for the candidate's suitability, whereas a Cover Letter is often a briefer introduction intended to motivate the employer to read the CV.



In a formal application letter, which section is specifically designed to reaffirm interest in the position and express a proactive willingness to attend an interview?
?
The closing paragraph
> The closing paragraph of an application letter is strategically used to reaffirm the candidate's interest, suggest a follow-up action like an interview, provide contact details, and thank the employer.



In a formal application letter, what is the specific purpose of the opening paragraph beyond simply introducing oneself?
?
To state the specific position and the source of the job advertisement
> The opening paragraph of an application letter is strategically designed to provide immediate context for the employer. It must state the exact position being applied for and identify the source of the advertisement (e.g., LinkedIn or a specific job board) to help the recruiter categorize the application correctly.


What is the primary function of a Cover Letter in relation to the CV or Resume it accompanies?
?
To introduce the candidate and encourage the employer to read the CV
