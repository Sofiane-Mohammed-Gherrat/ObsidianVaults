> “We are not just talking about diagnosis — we are talking about prevention.”

- The speaker emphasizes **predictive power** of AI — for example, predicting **Alzheimer’s 20+ years before symptoms**.
- AI can analyze **multi-modal data** — including **textual reports, images, videos, genetic data, and clinical history** — to detect subtle, early patterns.

> “This is not here’s a picture of a cat, here’s a description of a cat — this is a multi-dimensional video… three-dimensional, time-dependent, user-dependent.”

- This metaphor illustrates how AI handles **complex, dynamic data** — such as **medical imaging (MRI, PET, ultrasound)** — which may be **interpreted differently by different radiologists**.

> “There are other superhuman capabilities that we can go towards — and they require the next generation of algorithms, NVIDIA architectures, and data sets.”

> “How to include them in the workflow… how to pay for them…”

- The speaker **recognizes the practical barriers** to implementing AI:

  - **Cost and reimbursement** — healthcare systems need to pay for AI tools.
  - **Regulation and validation** — AI models must be clinically validated and approved (e.g., by FDA or CE).

### 5️⃣ **AI Can Reverse Disease — If We Act Early**
